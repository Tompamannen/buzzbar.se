Science

Backed by ingredients that work

Clean stimulation, steady focus, fewer jitters. Here’s the “why” behind Buzzbar — and how it’s different from quick spikes and sugary fixes.

Lift

Green-tea Caffeine

Steady focus from natural caffeine, without the sharp edge.

  • Target per bar: ~80 mg*
  • Source: green tea extract
  • Reason: pairs well with L-theanine for calmer alertness
Evidence snapshot

Moderate caffeine improves alertness and reaction time; when paired with L-theanine, studies report better task accuracy and fewer jitters (varies by dose and individual).†

Smooth

L-theanine

Promotes calm alertness and smooths caffeine’s lift.

  • Typical range: 100–200 mg
  • Effect: supports attention with relaxed focus
Evidence snapshot

Reviews note L-theanine increases alpha-wave activity (relaxed alertness). Combined with caffeine, it may reduce perceived stress and improve attention switching.

Hydrate

Electrolytes

Hydration supports cognition and endurance during long sessions.

  • Sodium • Potassium • Magnesium
  • Helps maintain fluid balance for mental stamina
Evidence snapshot

Even mild dehydration can impair mood and focus; electrolyte balance helps prevent those dips.

Resilience

Adaptogens

Stress-support so focus actually sticks (e.g., rhodiola, ashwagandha).

  • Targets perceived fatigue and stress reactivity
  • Complements caffeine’s alertness with composure
Evidence snapshot

Emerging research suggests some adaptogens reduce fatigue and support stress adaptation in healthy adults; effects vary with dose and personal response.

How it works — the quick version

  1. 1Lift: natural caffeine wakes up adenosine-fog — no harsh spike.
  2. 2Smooth: L-theanine modulates the lift → calm focus, fewer jitters.
  3. 3Hydrate: electrolytes keep the system balanced while you think.
  4. 4Resilience: adaptogens help your brain stay in “get-stuff-done” mode.
Why green tea instead of coffee?

Green tea has a gentler curve and plays nicely with L-theanine. Coffee’s great — we just wanted a carry-anywhere option with fewer spikes and no brewing.

When should I use Buzzbar?

Morning classes, deep-work blocks, pre-meeting boosts, travel days. If caffeine-sensitive, start with half.

Medical advice?

No — this is food, not medicine. If pregnant, nursing, or managing a condition, talk to your clinician before using caffeine or adaptogens.

*Targets shown during pre-release and may change as we finalize the formula. † Evidence snapshots are summary notes of published research; individual responses vary.

Our Story

Built for busy days.
